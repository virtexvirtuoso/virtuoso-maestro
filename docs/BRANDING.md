## Name Rationale

**Maestro** (Italian: "master") - The conductor who orchestrates all instruments into a harmonious performance. Just as a maestro coordinates musicians to create a symphony, Maestro orchestrates trading strategies through:

- **Walk-forward optimization** - Conducting the validation dance
- **Strategy ensemble** - Managing multiple trading instruments
- **Data harmonization** - Synchronizing market feeds
- **Performance analysis** - Critiquing and refining the composition

The name aligns with the Virtuoso family of products while emphasizing mastery and control over trading strategy development.

## Logo Concepts

### Primary Mark
A stylized conductor's baton forming an upward trend line, symbolizing:
- Market direction (upward movement)
- Precision control (the conductor's baton)
- Professional mastery (virtuoso performance)

### Secondary Mark
Musical notation (treble clef or bar) integrated with a candlestick chart pattern.

### Icon Variants
1. **Full logo**: Baton + "Maestro" wordmark
2. **Icon only**: Stylized baton
3. **Monogram**: "M" with baton element

## Typography

### Headings
- **Font**: Inter or SF Pro Display
- **Weight**: Bold (700) for H1-H2, Semibold (600) for H3-H4

### Body Text
- **Font**: Inter or SF Pro Text
- **Weight**: Regular (400), Medium (500) for emphasis

### Code / Monospace
- **Font**: JetBrains Mono or SF Mono
- **Weight**: Regular (400)

---

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Personality
- **Professional**: Institutional-quality analysis
- **Precise**: Data-driven, quantitative
- **Confident**: Backed by rigorous methodology
- **Accessible**: Technical but not intimidating

### Writing Style
- Use active voice
- Be concise and direct
- Lead with results, not process
- Quantify claims when possible

### Example Messaging

**Good**:
> "Maestro identified 5 strategies with Sharpe ratios above 1.5 across 10 walk-forward splits."

**Avoid**:
> "Our software might help you find some potentially profitable strategies."

---

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Use "Maestro" as the primary product name
- Use "Virtuoso Maestro" when referencing the full product suite
- Pair with the tagline in marketing materials
- Maintain color consistency with Virtuoso brand

### Don't
- Abbreviate to "Mae" or "M"
- Use "Filos" (deprecated name)
- Mix with competitor brand colors
- Use casual language in documentation
